Meet the Developer

Why Dumb Exists

Dumb was created to bypass the paywall—the growing wall between people and the news they need. We believe access should be earned by curiosity, not gated by subscriptions.

Behind the simple UI is a stubborn mission: unstick journalism from ad pixels and corporate choke points, surface the story without the squeeze, and make sure a reader in a dorm room has the same shot at the truth as someone with ten subscriptions.

We build with restraint and a bias for clarity. No dark patterns, no engagement traps, just a clean path from headline to understanding. Dumb is the small rebellion that keeps the web honest—one app, one article, one bypass at a time.

Every release is a conversation with our users: what’s blocked, what’s broken, what’s worth knowing right now. That feedback loop shapes our roadmap more than investor decks ever could.

We’re not trying to replace journalism. We’re trying to give it room to breathe—free from the friction that keeps readers out and keeps hard reporting unseen. If a story matters, it shouldn’t be hidden. Dumb is how we keep it in the light.
